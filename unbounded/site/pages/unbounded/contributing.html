Contributing to UNBOUNDED

1. Welcome

You are here. That is enough.

You do not need permission. You do not need experience. You do not need to ask. If you want to help build a platform that belongs to its community — truly, actually belongs — then you are already a contributor. The only thing left is to figure out what you want to build.

2. First Steps

If you are new, start here:

  1. WELCOME.md — a short hello and orientation to the project
  2. MANIFESTO.md — what we believe and why we are building this
  3. blueprint — the technical vision, architecture overview, and roadmap

Read them in order. They will tell you where we are, where we are going, and whether you want to come along.

Then browse the issues. Read some pull requests. Lurk in the community chat. Get a feel for how things move.

Then do something. Anything. Fix a typo in the docs. Answer a question someone asked in an issue. Propose a feature you want to build. The way to become a contributor is to contribute.

3. Ways to Contribute

Code — features, bug fixes, tests, performance, security. Any language, any layer of the stack.

Design — UI, visual identity, accessibility, UX research. Make things usable and beautiful.

Writing — documentation, blog posts, tutorials, copy. Help people understand what we are building and why.

Ideas — proposals, strategies, problem identification. Not everything is code. Sometimes the most valuable contribution is seeing the problem clearly.

Translations — bring UNBOUNDED to your language. The platform should speak to everyone.

Testing — break things on purpose. Report bugs. Write test cases. Make the project more robust.

Community — answer questions, welcome newcomers, organize events, facilitate discussions. The community is the product.

Art — music, illustration, animation, sound design. A platform has a soul. Help define ours.

If it helps the project, it counts. If you are unsure whether something counts, it counts.

4. The Contribution Flow

Propose

Open an issue or start a discussion. Say what you want to do and why. This is not permission-seeking — it is coordination. Someone else might be working on the same thing. Someone might have context that saves you time.

Discuss

Engage with feedback. Defend your approach or adjust it. The goal is not to win arguments — it is to find the best path forward together.

Build

Do the work. Work in the open if you can — a draft PR, a shared document, a branch. Let people see what you are doing as you do it.

Submit

Open a pull request or share your contribution. Gardeners will review it. They may ask questions or suggest changes. This is not rejection — it is collaboration.

Merge

Once the review is complete and any concerns are addressed, your contribution becomes part of UNBOUNDED. You are now in the history of the project permanently.

5. Communication

  • Community Chat — day-to-day discussion, Q&A, casual conversation
  • Community Calls — regular synchronous meetings for proposals, updates, and discussion (open to all)
  • Forum — longer-form discussions, proposals, governance topics
  • GitHub — issues, pull requests, project boards

6. Code of Conduct

Be excellent to each other. Full stop.

We do not have a long list of prohibited behaviors. We have one standard: treat people with respect. If you cannot do that, this is not the place for you.

If someone is making the community unsafe or unwelcoming, raise it in the community chat or contact a Wayfinder. Serious issues are handled through the consent process in the governance model.

7. Recognition

Every contributor is credited in the project's CONTRIBUTORS file. You can choose your name, your handle, or remain anonymous.

Major contributions are called out in the changelog. Design contributions are attributed in the interface. Translations credit the translator. Code contributions live forever in the git history.

If the project generates revenue, contributors share in it proportionally (see the governance model for details).

Your work here is your work. It goes in your portfolio. It goes on your resume. It is real.

8. Questions

Where do I ask?
- General questions: community chat
- Technical questions: GitHub issues (tag with question)
- Governance questions: forum
- Private concerns: contact a Wayfinder or Steward directly

What if I am stuck?
Ask. No one expects you to know everything. Everyone started somewhere.

What if I make a mistake?
You will. We all do. Fix it, learn from it, move on. The only real mistake is hiding a mistake.

What if I want to leave?
You can leave anytime. Your contributions remain credited. The door is always open if you want to come back.

You are here. That is enough. Now let's build something that matters.
