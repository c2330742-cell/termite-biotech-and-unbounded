Welcome.

What This Is

UNBOUNDED is a new media platform where information is entertainment, creation is consumption, and everyone is a broadcaster. No scripts. No gatekeepers. No passive consumption. It imagines a world where every session is unique, every viewer is also a creator, and deep knowledge is as gripping as the best drama you have ever watched.

It was born from the same soil as its sibling project — the Termite Research Initiative — a real, ongoing scientific research program based in the Philippines that isolates bacteria from termite guts to produce biofuel, natural pest control, and sustainable protein. Termites have been breaking down cellulose for 250 million years. They have solved problems we are still struggling with. That same spirit of looking to nature — and to each other — for better answers runs through everything here.

The termite project is the science. UNBOUNDED is the vision. Together they prove the same point: the best solutions are already out there, hidden in plain sight, waiting for someone to look.

This project does not ask you to choose between practicality and idealism. It asks you to hold both. The termite project shows what is possible when you look where others have not. UNBOUNDED shows what is possible when you imagine what others have not dared. They are two sides of the same coin — and this repository is where they meet.

The Two Gardens

The Termite Project — Practical Science

What it is: Real laboratory work — isolating and culturing bacteria from the guts of Macrotermes gilvus termites collected in Southern Luzon, Philippines. The goal was threefold: convert agricultural waste into ethanol, develop natural pest control formulations, and establish captive termite farming for protein and research.

Three interconnected research initiatives, one lab, one team, one vision:

1. Biofuel Research: 7 bacterial strains (TF-001 through TF-007) isolated from termite gut. A C-5 enzyme cocktail achieving 89.4% cellulose conversion. Co-culture fermentation (TF-001+TF-006) producing 10.8 g/L ethanol — 95% of theoretical yield. Scale-up to 5L bioreactor fed-batch reaching 12.5 g/L. A breakeven price of P23.23/L at 300,000 L annual production.

2. Natural Pest Control: 4 formulations tested with 88-100% efficacy against target pests. An essential oil blend achieving 100% mortality at just 250 uL/L. A US$1B+ annual market opportunity in Southeast Asia alone. Manufacturing guides, safety data sheets, and certification roadmaps already written.

3. Termite Farming: 3 M. gilvus colonies established with 86% growth rate. Protein content of 40-60% by dry weight — competitive with conventional protein sources. Thailand benchmark model producing 3,700 tons/year at ~3.4 USD/kg. Seven-part implementation plan covering facility design, colony management, processing, quality control, and distribution.

UNBOUNDED — Visionary Media

What it is: A new kind of media platform. Not a streaming service. Not social media. A living knowledge system where content is generated in real time, shaped by the people experiencing it, and designed to make you smarter — not numb.

The platform concept is called AEON (Always Evolving, Open, Networked). It uses a pantheon of AI models working together — Narrator, Visualist, Logician, Empath, Connector, Critic, Director — orchestrated by a meta-agent that allocates cognitive resources and ensures a coherent user experience. No two sessions are ever the same. You do not watch. You participate.

A Walking Tour

Here is the recommended path through the project. Each step builds on the last. You can skip around freely, but this order will give you the full picture of what this is, why it matters, and how to get involved.

Step 1: Read the Manifesto -> docs/MANIFESTO.md

The soul of the project.

This is where it all begins. 559 lines of declaration — a full-throated rejection of passive media and a vision for something alive in its place. It names the enemies: attention harvesting, algorithmic manipulation, the flattening of taste, scripted emptiness. It names what we are for: curiosity, participation, knowledge as entertainment, beauty that serves truth.

Read this first. Not because you need to agree with every word, but because you need to feel what this project is running toward. If it resonates, you belong here. If it does not, you will know honestly, and that is okay too.

The manifesto is the closest thing this project has to a sacred text. It was written from a place of deep frustration with the old media and deep hope for what could replace it. You can feel both in every paragraph.

Step 2: See the Blueprint -> docs/PLATFORM-BLUEPRINT.md

The mind of the project.

If the manifesto is the why, this is the how. 561 lines of technical architecture — ASCII system diagrams, data flow charts, model orchestration protocols, content moderation strategy, scaling roadmap from 5,000 to 500 million users, protocol specifications, risk matrices, and contribution tiers.

This document exists to answer the question: "Is this actually buildable?" The answer is yes — and here is exactly how. It covers the Director Agent state machine (4 phases: Blueprint, Generation, Interaction, Integration), the model loading strategy (always-hot vs demand-loaded), the provenance chain for every claim, the validator economy, and the phased infrastructure scaling plan.

This is for the builders and the skeptics. If you are the kind of person who needs to see the engineering before you believe, start here.

Step 3: Visit the Portal -> site/portal.html

The heart of the project — interactive.

Open this in a browser. It is not a document — it is an experience. A living web page with a guestbook, a knowledge map, ambient sound, and space to leave your voice. This is what UNBOUNDED feels like as a place, not just an idea.

The portal was designed to be the first thing a newcomer encounters. It does not explain the project — it shows you. The ambient sound creates atmosphere. The guestbook shows you that other people have been here before. The knowledge map invites exploration.

Sign the guestbook. Write your name. Tell us where you are from. Leave a message if you want. This is the first act of participation — the moment you stop being a visitor and start being part of the garden.

Step 4: Explore the Data -> site/dashboard.html

The evidence — real experiments.

This dashboard loads real CSV files from the termite project biofuel research and renders them as interactive charts. You can see the cellulase assay data, fermentation curves, glucose calibration regressions, and agricultural waste test results. The data is not simulated. These are real measurements from real lab work.

This is where the vision meets the ground. While UNBOUNDED dreams of the future of media, the termite project is in a lab in the Philippines, measuring real things, getting real results. 7 bacterial strains. 89.4% conversion. 10.8 g/L ethanol. 4 formulations. 3 colonies. The dashboard proves it.

The CSV files that power this dashboard live in the termite project repository at ../termite-projects/biofuel-research/data/. You can inspect them directly, run your own analysis, or contribute new experiments.

Step 5: Run the Gateway -> src/gateway.py

The CLI — type, explore.

The simplest way: run python run.py from the project root. This opens a menu where you can:
- [1] Launch the CLI Gateway
- [2] Start a web server on port 8080
- [3] Open the portal in your browser
- [4] Open the data dashboard
- [5] Open the landing page
- [6] Open the project folder

Or run python src/gateway.py directly for the full CLI experience — a 695-line Python program with 9 menu options covering: termite project summary, experimental data analysis (with linear regression, CSV parsing, and strain rankings), lab notebook browsing, UNBOUNDED vision exploration, calls to action, investor materials, cross-project connections, portal launcher, and help/about.

The gateway is the bridge. It is where the two projects — soil and screen — become one interface. It is also a good example of the code style and structure if you are a developer looking to contribute.

Step 6: Read the Governance -> GOVERNANCE.md

How we run.

Every ambitious project either builds governance intentionally or has it imposed by circumstance. This document outlines how UNBOUNDED makes decisions — who has a voice, how conflicts are resolved, how contributions are recognized, and how the project stays true to its principles as it grows.

The governance model covers:
- Decision-making processes (consensus-seeking, with escalation paths)
- Contributor roles and how to earn them
- Code of conduct and community standards
- How principles (no ads, no surveillance, etc.) are protected from dilution
- How the project evolves its own governance over time

Read it to understand not just what we are building, but how we are building it. A project that aims to replace gatekeepers cannot have gatekeepers of its own.

Step 7: Join the Garden -> Leave your voice in the portal

Open the portal (site/portal.html) and find the guestbook. Sign it. Tell us who you are, what brought you here, what you are curious about. This is the first act of participation. You are no longer consuming — you are contributing.

The garden grows with every voice added. Every signature in the guestbook is a data point that says "someone was here, someone cared, someone is paying attention." That matters more than you might think. A project is not a repository of files — it is a gathering of people. The guestbook is where that gathering begins.

You can also leave your mark in other ways: open an issue introducing yourself, fork the repo, share the project with someone who needs to see it. Every signal helps.

Step 8: Contribute -> CONTRIBUTING.md

Start building.

When you are ready to help — whether with code, design, writing, questions, or ideas — the contributing guide shows you how. It covers:
- Where to start based on your skills
- How to submit work (pull requests, issues, discussions)
- How to communicate (communication channels, meeting rhythms)
- How to find a role that fits your passions and availability
- How contributions are recognized and credited

You do not need to be an engineer. You need to care. We need writers, designers, musicians, philosophers, project managers, testers, translators, community builders, and people who just want to help. If you care about the mission, there is a place for you.

For Different Audiences

Not everyone needs to read everything. Here are curated paths for different kinds of visitors. Each path takes about 15-30 minutes and gives you exactly what you need.

"I am a developer"

You want to understand the architecture, see the code, and find where you can help.

Your path:
1. docs/PLATFORM-BLUEPRINT.md — 561 lines of technical specification. System diagrams, model orchestration, knowledge graph design, scaling roadmap. This is your primary document.
2. site/dashboard.html — see how real CSV data is loaded, parsed, and rendered as interactive charts using vanilla JavaScript. Good reference for the code style.
3. src/gateway.py — 695 lines of working Python CLI. Reads files, parses CSV, runs regressions, launches browsers. This is the most substantial code in the project. Study it, extend it, refactor it.
4. calls/FOR-DEVELOPERS.md — the technical call to action. Lists the stack challenges (real-time multi-model orchestration, knowledge graph, procedural content generation, co-creation tools, edge computing, decentralized distribution).
5. CONTRIBUTING.md — find your entry point and start building.

Current tech stack: Python 3, vanilla JavaScript, HTML5/CSS3, colorama for CLI, csv module for data. No frameworks yet. No build tools. You can help decide the architecture before it hardens.

The vision calls for: Rust (orchestration layer), Neo4j or Dgraph (knowledge graph), vLLM/TGI (model inference), WebGPU (edge inference), libp2p (P2P mesh), WebRTC (real-time streaming), IPFS (content addressing). The foundation is being laid now.
