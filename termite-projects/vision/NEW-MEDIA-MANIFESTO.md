### 2. THE CONCEPT: AEON

**Name:** AEON (Always Evolving, Open, Networked)

**Core Mechanic:** Every piece of content on AEON is generated or co-created in real time. There are no recordings. There are no reruns. There is no "season two" of a show — there are infinite sessions of a living concept, each one different because the audience, the data, the world, and the knowledge graph have all changed since the last time.

**The User Experience:**

You open AEON. There is no library of thumbnails. There is no "continue watching." There is a single question, rendered beautifully across your screen:

*"What do you want to become?"*

Not what do you want to watch. What do you want to *become*.

You type: "I want to understand quantum computing."

AEON responds instantly. Not with a search result. Not with a Wikipedia article. With a session — a live, procedurally generated experience that adapts to your current knowledge level, your learning style, your emotional state, your pace. A guide — part teacher, part storyteller, part visualizer — takes you through the material. You can interrupt. You can ask questions. You can say "I don't understand" and the entire presentation reconfigures itself, finds a new metaphor, a new angle, a new way in.

Or you say: "I want to be entertained."

AEON asks: "What kind of mind do you want to meet today? A philosopher? A scientist? A heretic? A poet? A civilization from ten thousand years ago?"

You choose. A dialogue begins. Not a pre-recorded lecture. A real-time synthesis of everything humanity knows, filtered through the lens of the mind you chose to meet. You are not watching a show. You are *in* the conversation.

Or you say: "I want to build something."

AEON says: "Show me your hands." Your camera activates. An AI master craftsperson appears beside you — in AR, in VR, on your screen — and guides you through building a piece of furniture, a circuit board, a poem, a business plan, a garden. You do not watch. You *do*. By the end, you have made something real.

**No two visits to AEON are the same.** The platform remembers you but never repeats itself. Every session is a unique collaboration between the user, the knowledge graph, the generative models, and the global community of minds who have shaped the content before you.

AEON is not a streaming service. It is a living library, a forge, a garden, a conversation. It is the opposite of television.

### 3. HOW IT WORKS — TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

#### 3.1 The Content Engine (Multi-Model Orchestration)

AEON does not rely on a single AI. That would be a single point of failure, a single perspective, a single flavor of intelligence. Instead, AEON orchestrates a **pantheon of models**:

- **The Narrator** — a large language model specialized in narrative generation, pacing, voice, and emotional arc. It does not write scripts. It generates live narrative frameworks that adapt in real time.
- **The Visualist** — a multi-modal diffusion model that generates imagery, video, 3D environments, and visual metaphors on the fly. Every visual is unique to that session.
- **The Logician** — a reasoning model that ensures factual accuracy, logical consistency, and knowledge graph integrity. It fact-checks the Narrator in real time.
- **The Empath** — a model trained on affective computing, reading user biometrics (with consent), tone, engagement levels, and emotional state to adjust pacing, difficulty, and tone.
- **The Connector** — a model that links current session content to the global knowledge graph, finding unexpected connections, surfacing relevant prior sessions, and weaving the user's personal journey into the broader tapestry.
- **The Critic** — a model that evaluates the generated content for quality, novelty, and alignment with platform values. It rejects anything derivative, manipulative, or low-effort.

These models work in concert through a **Director Agent** — a meta-model that allocates cognitive resources, decides which model leads at any moment, and ensures a coherent user experience.

#### 3.2 Real-Time Audience Participation Layer

For live sessions (the default), users are not alone. They are part of a **viewing collective** — other minds experiencing the same session simultaneously, or asynchronously contributing to the same knowledge thread.

- **Live Voting:** The audience can redirect the session. "Go deeper on that point." "Give an example." "Challenge that assumption." The Director Agent weighs these inputs and adjusts the flow.
- **Parallel Threads:** Users can split off into private explorations while the main session continues, then rejoin with their findings shared back to the collective.
- **Ghost Presence:** You can see the traces of previous users who took this same journey — where they asked questions, what they built, what they discovered. The platform is haunted by the minds that came before you.

#### 3.3 The Knowledge Graph

This is the beating heart of AEON. Every session, every interaction, every question, every creation feeds into a **living knowledge graph** — a hyper-dimensional map of human understanding that grows with every session.

- Nodes represent concepts, skills, people, ideas, artifacts, questions, and connections.
- Edges represent relationships: "builds upon," "contradicts," "was inspired by," "is a prerequisite for."
- The graph is not static. It evolves as new knowledge is generated, as connections are discovered, as users contribute their own insights.

The graph allows AEON to:
- Know exactly where you are in your learning journey and what comes next.
- Find surprising connections between domains (e.g., linking neural networks to mycelial networks to supply chain logistics).
- Generate content that is always building on something real, never凭空 (out of thin air).

#### 3.4 Verification and Reputation System

Quality is the moat. The old internet gave us misinformation because attention was the only metric. AEON uses a **multi-layered verification system**:

- **AI Cross-Verification:** The Logician model cross-references every factual claim against the knowledge graph, primary sources, and the peer-reviewed literature. Unverified claims are flagged, not censored.
- **Human Validator Network:** A distributed network of domain experts who review and rate sessions, sources, and contributions. Validators earn reputation and platform stake for their work.
- **Community Dais:** Users can challenge content. A challenged claim enters a structured debate process — AI-moderated, evidence-required, resolution-tracked. The outcome updates the knowledge graph.
- **Source Provenance:** Every claim in every session is traced back to its origin. You can drill down from a statement to its source material — a paper, a dataset, a primary document, a verified expert.

#### 3.5 Infrastructure

Hybrid architecture:
- **Core Graph** — run on a combination of dedicated servers and a permissioned blockchain for provenance and validator economics.
- **Edge Inference** — generation happens at the edge where possible. Your device runs local models for personalization, privacy, and responsiveness. Only what needs the full orchestration reaches the cloud.
- **Peer-to-Peer Session Relay** — for live collective sessions, we use WebRTC and P2P meshes to minimize latency and server costs.

### 4. CONTENT CATEGORIES

These are not "shows" in the old sense. They are **session types** — frameworks that the AI director uses to structure the experience. Each one is infinite in variation. Users can also invent new session types, which become part of the platform's growing vocabulary of knowledge transfer forms.

Each session type has a **canonical form** (a default structure the AI follows) and an **infinite surface** (the set of all possible variations). You might experience a session type a hundred times and never see the same structure twice. The form is a constraint that enables creativity, not a cage that limits it.

#### 4.1 "The Unwelcome"

*What the gatekeepers kept from you.*

A forensic deep-dive into suppressed knowledge. Not conspiracy. Evidence. The history of alternative energy technologies that were buried. The medical research that contradicted pharmaceutical narratives. The political events that were scrubbed from the record. The indigenous knowledge systems that colonialism erased. Each session is a detective story — the host (an AI persona embodying a relentless researcher) follows the thread from official narrative to hidden truth. The audience participates by bringing their own sources, which the Logician immediately vets and integrates. Every session ends with actionable knowledge — something you can verify yourself, something you can use.

**The Unwelcome protocol:** Every session begins with a "knowledge map" of what is officially known versus what is suppressed. The host explains the mechanism of suppression — was it economic? Political? Accidental? The session does not just teach you the hidden knowledge; it teaches you how knowledge gets hidden. You learn the epistemology of suppression. After the session, you are not just informed — you are inoculated against future suppression.

**Use case:** A session on the history of free energy research reveals not just the science but the patent battles, the corporate buyouts, the researchers who disappeared from public view. You leave with a reading list, a set of verifiable claims, and the forensic skills to investigate your own suppressed questions.

**Why it works on AEON:** Because the Logician ensures every claim is sourced. Because the audience brings their own research and the system validates it in real time. Because the knowledge graph tracks each claim's provenance, creating an ever-growing web of verifiable suppressed knowledge that becomes harder to ignore with every session.

#### 4.2 "Build Everything"

*Learn by doing, alongside a master.*

A master craftsperson — blacksmith, programmer, carpenter, chef, musician, gardener, engineer, poet — appears beside you. You have your materials. They have their knowledge. The session proceeds in real time as you build together. The AI master watches your progress, corrects your technique, explains the原理 (principles) behind every step. By the end, you have not watched someone build something. You built it yourself. The knowledge graph tracks your skill development across sessions, building a portfolio of demonstrated competence.

**The Build Everything protocol:** The system first assesses your current skill level through a diagnostic build. Then it generates a project that sits at the edge of your competence — hard enough to challenge you, easy enough that you can succeed with guidance. The master adapts their teaching style to your learning style. Some people need to understand the theory before they do. Some need to fail first and then learn why. The Empath model detects which kind of learner you are and adjusts accordingly.

**Why it works on AEON:** Because the Visualist can generate any material, any tool, any environment. You do not need a forge to learn blacksmithing. You do not need a forest to learn woodworking. The session generates a photorealistic or AR environment with everything you need. The barrier to entry is zero. The ceiling is mastery.

**Skill certification:** At the end of each build, the system evaluates your work and issues a verifiable skill credential — linked to the session recording, the master's assessment, and the knowledge graph's skill taxonomy. These credentials accumulate into a public skill portfolio that you control. Imagine a resume where every skill is backed by a video of you actually doing the thing, validated by an AI master and the platform's reputation system.

#### 4.3 "The Living Debate"

*AI-moderated Socratic dialogue, live and unfiltered.*

Two (or more) AI personas embody opposing positions on a complex topic. The audience proposes challenges, questions, and lines of inquiry. The AI moderator enforces logical rigor, prevents fallacies, highlights common ground, and deepens the exploration. The debate does not aim for "winning" — it aims for understanding. When the audience is divided, the debate branches — different groups explore different lines, then reconvene and share insights. The knowledge graph captures every argument, counterargument, and synthesis.

**The Debate protocol:** Before the session begins, the audience votes on the topic and the specific framing. The AI generates an initial "argument map" — a visual representation of the claims, evidence, and logical structure of both sides. As the debate proceeds, this map fills in with new arguments, evidence, and counterarguments. At any point, any participant can say "I need to see the evidence for that claim" and the Logician pulls up the source. By the end, the audience has not just watched a debate — they have co-created the most comprehensive argument map on that topic that exists.

**Use case:** "Is consciousness an emergent property of matter or something more?" Two AI personas — a materialist neuroscientist and a panpsychist philosopher — debate for forty minutes. The audience interjects with questions, challenges, their own experiences. The argument map grows dense with references to Integrated Information Theory, Global Workspace Theory, Buddhist philosophy, quantum consciousness hypotheses. Every claim is sourced. Every objection is logged. The session does not settle the question — it makes you an expert on all sides of it.

**The Socratic layer:** The moderator does not just keep the debate fair. It actively pushes both sides toward deeper rigor. If a persona makes a weak argument, the moderator says "That argument has been criticized on the following grounds — please respond." The debate gets better in real time because the moderator holds both sides to the highest standard.

#### 4.4 "Memory Palace"

*Walk through the history of an idea.*

You enter a 3D/VR environment that IS an idea. The concept of democracy built as a city you walk through, each building a different era, a different thinker, a different revolution. The concept of zero as a desert landscape where you discover the void. You move at your own pace. You open doors that interest you. The Visualist generates the environment in real time based on the knowledge graph's understanding of the idea's history and connections. This is immersive education as architecture.

**The Memory Palace protocol:** The system selects an idea with rich intellectual history. It maps the idea's evolution onto a spatial metaphor — a city (for concepts with distinct historical periods), a landscape (for concepts with branching interpretations), a building (for concepts with layered depth). Each room or region corresponds to a stage in the idea's development. You can walk in any direction. You can open any door. Each room contains a mini-session — a dialogue with the thinker who owned that room, a visualization of the core insight, a challenge that tests your understanding. The longer you explore, the more the palace reveals its hidden connections — secret passages between rooms that represent the non-obvious links between different eras of the idea.

**Why it is revolutionary:** Because ideas are not linear. They do not unfold in a straight line from past to present. They loop, they branch, they are forgotten and rediscovered. The Memory Palace spatializes this nonlinearity. You experience the idea the way it actually evolved — as a network of discoveries, setbacks, rediscoveries, and transformations. No textbook can do this. No video can do this. Only a living 3D environment, generated in real time from a knowledge graph, can make the history of an idea something you can walk through.

#### 4.5 "The Oracle"

*Ask any question. Receive not an answer, but a journey.*

The Oracle does not give direct answers. It gives you a path to discover the answer yourself. Ask "What is the meaning of life?" and the Oracle sends you on a curated exploration through philosophy, neuroscience, art, religion, and personal stories — each session a different path, each one leading you toward your own conclusion. The Oracle remembers your journey and never sends you down the same path twice.

**The Oracle protocol:** The question is parsed into its conceptual components. The knowledge graph is queried for all relevant domains, perspectives, and unresolved tensions. The Director constructs a journey — a sequence of mini-sessions that form a coherent narrative arc. The journey has a beginning (framing the question, why it matters), a middle (exploring different answers from different traditions), and an end (synthesizing, returning the question to you with new depth). The Oracle does not answer your question. It makes you worthy of your question.

**Use case:** You ask "What is time?" The Oracle does not give you the dictionary definition. It starts with Augustine's confession ("What then is time? If no one asks me, I know; if I wish to explain, I know not"). Then it moves to Newton's absolute time, Einstein's relative time, the thermodynamic arrow, the psychological experience of time, the Buddhist concept of eternal now. Each stop on the journey is a session within the session — a dialogue, a visualization, a thought experiment. At the end, you do not have the answer. But you understand why the question matters, and you have the tools to pursue it further. The Oracle has not closed the question. It has opened it in the most productive way possible.

#### 4.6 "Fringe"

*The edge of human knowledge, live.*

A session that begins at the frontier of a field — dark matter, consciousness, quantum gravity, the origins of life — and then pushes beyond. Not settled science. The questions that have no answers yet. The hypotheses that might be wrong but are worth exploring. The audience interacts with active researchers (real humans, verified) who present their work in real time. The AI translates their specialized knowledge into accessible exploration. Every Fringe session contributes real data to the knowledge graph — not answers, but better questions.

**The Fringe protocol:** A real researcher — a human expert who has passed platform verification — presents their current work at the edge of their field. The Narrator model assists with presentation and pacing. The Logician ensures that what is known is clearly separated from what is hypothesized. The audience asks questions, proposes alternative interpretations, and in some cases contributes computational resources or data analysis to the researcher's work. The session is not a lecture. It is a collaborative exploration.

**The Fringe marketplace:** Researchers apply to present. The community votes on which sessions get platform resources. The most promising research attracts collaborators, funding, and attention from the broader community. Fringe sessions have led to actual scientific collaborations — dispersed teams formed during sessions, data shared, papers co-authored. The platform becomes not just a media experience but a scientific instrument. The line between watching research and doing research disappears.

**Why it works on AEON:** Because the AI translation layer makes cutting-edge research accessible without dumbing it down. Because the audience brings diverse expertise that the researcher can draw on. Because the session is recorded, sourced, and added to the knowledge graph — creating a permanent record of the frontier that future explorers can build upon.

#### 4.7 "The Mirror"

*You are the content.*

A session that turns inward. With your consent, AEON builds a session about YOU — your beliefs, your patterns, your blind spots, your potential. The Empath model, combined with the Logician, creates a structured exploration of your own mind. What do you assume? What do you avoid? What would you think if you were born somewhere else? This is not therapy. This is cognitive cartography — mapping the territory of your own consciousness with you as both explorer and territory.

**The Mirror protocol:** The session begins with a series of carefully designed probes — questions, scenarios, challenges — that reveal the structure of your thinking. Not to judge it. To map it. The Empath model observes your responses, your hesitations, your emotional reactions. The Logician identifies patterns — cognitive biases, unchallenged assumptions, areas of avoidant thinking. The Director constructs a live map of your cognitive landscape, projected in real time. You can see your own mind as a territory with explored regions, unexplored regions, and regions you have been avoiding.

**The Mirror challenge:** The session can optionally enter "deep mirror" mode, where it simulates alternative versions of you — the you that was born in a different culture, the you that held different beliefs, the you that made different choices. These alternative selves engage in dialogue with you, revealing the contingency of your own identity. You are not who you think you are. You are who you became by accident, by circumstance, by choices you barely remember making. The Mirror shows you the you that could have been — and in doing so, shows you the you that can still become.

**The risk and the safeguard:** The Mirror is potentially the most powerful and dangerous session type. It is strictly opt-in, with multiple consent layers. Session data is processed locally, encrypted, and never shared without explicit permission. The Mirror does not diagnose. It does not pathologize. It maps. It reveals. It is a tool for self-understanding, not self-criticism. The Empath model is specifically trained to maintain a supportive, non-judgmental tone even when revealing uncomfortable truths.

#### 4.8 "World Forge"

*Create a civilization.*

A god-game where you design a culture, a society, a system of values, a technology tree — and then watch the AI simulate its development over centuries. Your choices have consequences. Your assumptions are stress-tested. The simulation reveals the hidden dynamics of your own beliefs about how societies work. Other users can visit your world, interact with its inhabitants (AI-generated citizens with their own emergent behaviors), and learn from what you built. The best worlds become permanent nodes in the knowledge graph.

**The World Forge protocol:** You start with the primordial choices: what does this society value above all else? Is it stability? Exploration? Knowledge? Power? Harmony? You set the environment — a lush valley, a desert crossroads, an archipelago, a hostile tundra. You define the founding myths, the taboos, the sacred texts. Then you press play. The simulation runs — generations pass, civilizations rise and fall, technologies are discovered or abandoned, wars are fought or avoided. You can intervene at any point — introducing a new idea, a natural disaster, a foreign contact. The simulation adapts. The AI generates the history in real time, driven by the knowledge graph's understanding of how societies actually work.

**The emergent education:** World Forge is not a game. It is a sandbox for understanding social dynamics. You will learn more about game theory, anthropology, political science, and environmental determinism in a single session than in a semester of lectures. Because you are not learning about these things. You are experiencing them. You designed a peaceful society that was conquered because you did not invest in defense. You designed a technological utopia that collapsed because you neglected social cohesion. You designed a theocracy that lasted a thousand years but stagnated. Each failure teaches you something about your own assumptions.

**The social layer:** Other users can visit your world as "anthropologists" — observing your civilization, interacting with its inhabitants, writing field reports. These reports become part of the world's history in the knowledge graph. Your world becomes a living experiment that the entire community can learn from.

You are learning about the universal pattern that expresses itself through both.

**The Crossroads effect:** Users report that Crossroads sessions change the way they see everything. You start seeing isomorphisms everywhere. The world becomes more connected, more meaningful, more comprehensible.

**User-generated crosswords:** Users can submit their own domain pairs for Crossroads sessions. The Connector model evaluates them for potential depth. The best submissions become featured sessions. Over time, the Crossroads catalog grows into a map of all the deep structural patterns that underlie reality — a kind of unified field theory of knowledge.

**The Time Capsule protocol:** You specify a point in your past — "me at sixteen, before I made that mistake." The system uses your personal knowledge graph history (your choices, your beliefs at that time, your developmental trajectory) to reconstruct the cognitive state of your past self. ### 7. TECHNOLOGY STACK

**What actually makes this possible in 2026?**

The convergence of several technological trends that were, until recently, separate tracks: open-weight language models that can run on consumer hardware, diffusion models that can generate coherent video in real time, graph databases that can store and query billions of relationships, peer-to-peer protocols that enable decentralized coordination, and edge computing that puts AI in your pocket. Five years ago, any one of these was science fiction. Today, they are all real. Tomorrow, they are AEON.

#### Foundation Models (Open-Source)

- **LLaMA 4 / Mistral Large / Qwen 3.5** — for the Narrator, the Logician, and the Critic. Open-weight models that can be fine-tuned and run locally. No vendor lock-in. No API dependency. In 2026, these models achieve GPT-4-class reasoning on consumer hardware (24GB VRAM). They are the backbone of the system. They are free. They are sovereign.
- **Stable Diffusion 4 / Flux.1 Pro / Sora-class video models** — for the Visualist. Multi-modal generation that improves with every open-source release. SD4 can generate consistent characters and environments across multiple frames. Flux.1 Pro achieves cinema-quality output at 1024p. Sora-class models generate coherent minute-long video clips from text descriptions. Combined, they give the Visualist a palette that rivals any production studio — at a fraction of the cost, in real time, generated uniquely for each session.
- **LLaVA / CogVLM** — for multi-modal understanding, enabling the system to see what you're building, watch your expressions, and respond to visual context. The Visualist does not just generate images. It understands them. It sees the confusion on your face and asks if you need clarification. It sees the book you are holding and integrates it into the session. The boundary between the digital and physical dissolves.
- **OpenCog / Hyperon** — for the knowledge graph reasoning layer. Probabilistic logic that enables the Connector to find non-obvious relationships. These are not neural networks. They are symbolic reasoning systems that work alongside neural networks — the left brain to the Narrator's right brain. They ensure that connections are logically valid, not just statistically plausible.
- **Whisper / SeamlessM4T** — for voice interaction. AEON is voice-native if you want it to be. Whisper achieves near-human transcription accuracy in 99 languages. SeamlessM4T enables real-time translation between languages. A session in Japanese, delivered to you in English, with the speaker's original voice preserved in tone and cadence. Language is no longer a barrier to knowledge.

#### Real-Time Inference

The hard problem: generating high-quality text and images fast enough that the user experiences the session as a real-time conversation, not a loading screen. The solution is a tiered inference architecture.

- **vLLM / TensorRT-LLM** — for low-latency LLM inference. Target: <500ms per generation step for interactive feel. PagedAttention in vLLM enables efficient KV-cache management across many concurrent sessions. TensorRT-LLM compiles models to CUDA graphs for minimal latency. With speculative decoding (a small draft model proposes tokens, the large model validates), we achieve 2-3x throughput improvement without quality loss.
- **GGML / llama.cpp** — for on-device inference. Your phone runs the Empath and local personalization models. No cloud needed for basic operation. With Q4_K_M quantization, a 7B model fits in 4GB of RAM and generates at 30+ tokens per second on a modern phone. The Empath model processes biometric data entirely on-device. The cloud never sees your face, hears your voice, or reads your heart rate.
- **ComfyUI / custom diffusion pipelines** — for real-time visual generation with control nets for consistency. LCM-LoRA (Latent Consistency Models) reduces diffusion to 1-4 steps instead of 50. A high-quality image in 200ms. A video frame in 500ms. ControlNet ensures that characters, environments, and objects maintain visual consistency across generations. The visual world of your session does not flicker between styles. It holds together, coherent and stable.

#### Edge Computing

- **Local-first architecture** — core session logic runs on-device. Models are downloaded and updated incrementally. The cloud is used for orchestration, heavy computation, and graph synchronization. This means AEON works offline. On a plane. In a cabin in the woods. In a region with no internet infrastructure. The experience degrades gracefully — the Narrator still works, the Empath still works, the knowledge graph still works (your local slice of it). Only the collaborative features require a connection.
- **WebGPU / DirectML / Metal** — for on-device GPU acceleration across platforms. No specialized hardware required for basic sessions. WebGPU brings GPU compute to the browser. DirectML accelerates Windows devices. Metal handles Apple Silicon. Between them, any device manufactured after 2023 can run AEON's local models at interactive speeds.
- **Compression techniques** — quantization (GGUF, AWQ), distillation, and speculative decoding to fit capable models on consumer hardware. GGUF (GGML Universal Format) compresses models to 2-8 bits per parameter with minimal quality loss. AWQ (Activation-aware Weight Quantization) further optimizes for the weights that matter most. Distillation trains smaller "student" models to replicate larger "teacher" models. Combined, we fit a 70B-class model's quality into a 7B model's footprint.
- **Incremental model updates** — models are updated over the air in small patches. No 10GB downloads. The system learns what models your usage patterns require and pre-caches them during idle time. Your device becomes smarter the more you use it, without you doing anything.

#### Knowledge Graph Database

- **Neo4j + custom vector extensions** — for the main graph structure. Relationships are as important as nodes. Neo4j's property graph model natively supports the rich relationship types that AEON requires (builds_on, contradicts, inspired_by, prerequisite, etc.). Custom vector extensions enable similarity-search across the graph — finding concepts that are structurally similar even if they are textually unrelated.
- **Pinecone / Qdrant / Weaviate** — for vector search across the graph. Semantic similarity queries for the Connector. When the Connector needs to find "concepts that relate to fungal networks the way X relates to Y," it uses vector search on the graph's embedding space. This enables cross-domain analogical reasoning at scale.
- **Local-first graph sync** — your personal knowledge graph is stored locally, synced to the global graph when you choose. Based on CRDTs (Conflict-free Replicated Data Types) for offline-first operation. CRDTs mean that your local edits and someone else's local edits can be merged automatically without conflicts. The graph is always consistent, even when it is distributed.
- **Graph visualization engine** — the knowledge graph is not just a database. It is an interface. Users can explore the graph visually, zooming in on concept clusters, following connection trails, discovering unexpected relationships. The graph becomes a navigable landscape of knowledge — a map of everything the platform knows, which you can wander like a garden.

#### Decentralized / Distributed

- **IPFS / Ceramic Network** — for content addressing and session persistence. Sessions are not stored on central servers; they are referenced by their content hash. This means no single entity can delete a session, censor a creator, or modify the historical record. Content is addressed by what it is, not where it is. It cannot be taken down. It can only be forgotten — and even that is difficult on a distributed network.
- **Libp2p** — for P2P session relay. Live collective sessions use peer-to-peer mesh networking. Instead of routing all traffic through central servers, participants connect directly to each other. This reduces latency, bandwidth costs, and central points of failure. A session with 1,000 participants uses 1,000 peer-to-peer connections rather than 1,000 connections to a server farm.
- **Holochain or similar agent-centric framework** — evaluated for validator reputation and governance. Not a blockchain in the traditional sense — no proof-of-work waste. A distributed ledger of contributions and validations. Holochain's agent-centric model means each user maintains their own chain of actions. Validation is distributed and probabilistic. No global consensus is needed. This scales better than traditional blockchains and avoids the environmental cost.
- **AT Protocol / ActivityPub** — for interoperability with the broader fediverse. AEON sessions can be shared, embedded, and discussed across decentralized platforms. Your AEON profile is your fediverse identity. Your discoveries can be posted to Mastodon. Your session templates can be discovered through federated search. AEON is not a walled garden. It is a node in the larger decentralized web.
- **Content distribution network (P2P)** — frequently accessed session templates and model weights are cached across the peer network. Popular content replicates automatically to the edge. Unpopular content remains in cold storage on IPFS. The network self-optimizes for access patterns without central coordination.

#### Additional Infrastructure

- **WebRTC** — for real-time audio/video/data channels in live sessions. WebRTC handles the bidirectional streaming of AI-generated audio, user voice, real-time video, and session data. With SVC (Scalable Video Coding), participants with different bandwidths receive different quality levels. No one is left behind because of a slow connection.
- **WebAssembly** — for running models and processing in-browser with near-native performance. WASM allows LLM inference and diffusion in the browser without plugins. llama.cpp compiled to WASM runs at 60-70% of native speed — fast enough for basic sessions. The web client is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought.
- **SQLite / DuckDB** — for local data persistence and analytics. SQLite stores your personal session history, preference models, and local knowledge graph. DuckDB enables analytical queries on your learning patterns — how many sessions have you completed? What domains have you explored? Where are your knowledge gaps? Your personal analytics are computed locally, never sent to a server.
- **Rust + Tauri** — for the desktop client. Performance-critical components in Rust. UI layer in web technologies. Tauri produces binaries that are 10-100x smaller than Electron equivalents. Memory usage is a fraction. Startup time is under a second. The desktop client is as responsive as a native application because its core is native.
- **React Native / Flutter** — for mobile clients with native performance. React Native provides JavaScript-level development speed with native rendering. Flutter provides pixel-level control and consistent rendering across platforms. Both options are maintained, allowing developers to choose their preferred paradigm. The mobile client includes hardware acceleration for local models via GPU delegate APIs.
- **End-to-end encryption layer** — all communication between clients, between client and graph, and between session participants is encrypted with modern AEAD ciphers (ChaCha20-Poly1305). Forward secrecy ensures that past sessions cannot be decrypted even if future keys are compromised. Encryption is not a feature. It is a default.
