# Colony Management Protocols for Termite Farming

## Executive Summary
This document establishes protocols for the collection, establishment, maintenance, and harvesting of termite colonies for commercial farming operations. Based on sustainable practices from Thai palm weevil farming and adapted for termite-specific requirements.

## 1. Species Selection & Sourcing

### 1.1 Target Species
**Primary Species:**
- *Macrotermes bellicosus* - Large colonies, high fungus production
- *Macrotermes carbonarius* - Tropical species, good growth rates
- *Macrotermes goliath* - Largest species, high biomass yield

**Secondary Species:**
- *Odontotermes obesus* - Fungus-growing termites
- *Microtermes* spp. - Smaller but prolific colonies

### 1.2 Sustainable Wild Collection Protocol
**Collection Quotas:**
- Maximum 10% of wild colonies per area annually
- Minimum distance between collection sites: 500 meters
- Rotation of collection areas (3-year cycle)

**Collection Methods:**
1. **Identification Phase (1-2 days)**
   - Locate active mounds with surface activity
   - Identify mound age (>2 years preferred)
   - Assess colony size via surface workers (target: >10,000)

2. **Extraction Phase (2-4 hours)**
   - Use specialized extraction tools
   - Collect queen, king, soldiers, workers, and fungus comb
   - Minimize disturbance to mound structure
   - Leave 30% of colony for regeneration

3. **Transport Phase (2-4 hours)**
   - Use climate-controlled containers (26-30°C, 70-80% RH)
   - Provide substrate (damp soil, wood fragments)
   - Minimize light exposure
   - Transport within 24 hours of collection

### 1.3 Quarantine Procedures
**Duration:** 14-21 days
**Isolation Requirements:**
- Separate building from main farm
- Dedicated equipment and staff
- Negative air pressure (if possible)
- Daily health monitoring

**Health Assessment:**
- Visual inspection for parasites/disease
- Mortality rate tracking (acceptable: <5%)
- Behavioral observation (activity levels)
- Microbiological testing if issues arise

## 2. Colony Establishment Protocol

### 2.1 Initial Housing Setup
**Nest Box Preparation:**
- Substrate: 50% topsoil, 30% sand, 20% organic matter
- Moisture content: 25-30% (squeeze test: no water, holds shape)
- Depth: 18-24 inches minimum
- pH: 6.5-7.5

**Introduction Procedure:**
1. Prepare nest box 24 hours before introduction
2. Create depression in substrate center
3. Gently transfer colony material (workers, soldiers, brood)
4. Place queen/king pair in depression
5. Add fungus comb pieces (if farming Termitomyces)
6. Cover lightly with substrate
7. Add initial wood pieces for nutrition
8. Close lid with ventilation mesh

### 2.2 Establishment Period (Weeks 1-4)
**Week 1:**
- Minimal disturbance (observation only)
- Maintain optimal conditions (28°C, 75% RH)
- Monitor surface activity daily
- Check for tunnel construction

**Week 2:**
- First feeding with fresh hardwood
- Assess nest construction progress
- Monitor worker activity levels
- Record any mortality

**Week 3:**
- Second feeding
- Measure tunnel expansion
- Check fungus comb development (if applicable)
- Adjust humidity if needed

**Week 4:**
- Full assessment of establishment success
- Criteria for success:
  - Worker activity >50% of initial
  - Visible tunnel construction
  - No signs of disease/parasites
  - Queen visible and active

### 2.3 Success Criteria & Troubleshooting

| Success Metric | Target | Intervention if Not Met |
|----------------|--------|-------------------------|
| Worker survival | >80% | Check climate, food, substrate |
| Tunnel construction | Visible by week 2 | Verify substrate moisture |
| Queen activity | Visible monthly | Assess stress factors |
| Fungus comb | Growing by week 4 | Check inoculation quality |

## 3. Colony Maintenance Protocols

### 3.1 Daily Monitoring
**Environmental Checks:**
- Temperature: 26-30°C (record 3× daily)
- Humidity: 70-80% RH (record 3× daily)
- CO₂ levels: <1000 ppm (continuous monitoring)
- Light: Minimal (dim red light for observations only)

**Colony Health Indicators:**
- Surface worker activity (count per minute)
- Foraging trails (presence/absence)
- Fungus comb color (cream/tan = healthy)
- Brood presence (eggs, larvae, pupae)
- Waste disposal patterns

### 3.2 Feeding Schedule
**Primary Substrate:**
- Hardwood logs: Oak, maple, eucalyptus, or local hardwoods
- Size: 6-12 inch diameter, 3 ft length
- Quantity: 1 log per 10-15 workers per week
- Rotation: Replace when 50% consumed

**Supplemental Feeding:**
- Cellulose sources: Cardboard, paper (processed)
- Nitrogen supplements: Dried leaves, grass
- Mineral supplements: Calcium, magnesium (quarterly)

**Feeding Protocol:**
1. Remove old substrate (worn logs)
2. Inspect feeding area for pests/disease
3. Place new logs in designated areas
4. Monitor consumption rates
5. Record feeding dates and quantities

### 3.3 Environmental Management
**Temperature Control:**
- Optimal: 28°C ± 2°C
- Winter: Increase heating if below 24°C
- Summer: Increase ventilation if above 32°C
- Emergency: Backup systems activate at 22°C/34°C

**Humidity Control:**
- Optimal: 75% RH ± 5%
- Low humidity: Increase misting frequency
- High humidity: Increase ventilation
- Drainage: Ensure no standing water

**Air Quality:**
- CO₂: <1000 ppm (continuous monitoring)
- Ammonia: <25 ppm (weekly testing)
- Dust: MERV-13 filtration on intake
- Mold: Monthly spore counts

### 3.4 Health Management
**Weekly Health Assessment:**
1. Visual inspection of surface activity
2. Mortality count (acceptable: <2% weekly)
3. Check for parasites (mites, nematodes)
4. Assess fungus comb health (if applicable)
5. Record brood development stage

**Disease Prevention:**
- Quarantine new colonies (21 days minimum)
- Sterilize equipment between colonies
- Maintain optimal conditions (reduces stress)
- Remove dead termites immediately
- Monitor for fungal infections (green mold)

**Treatment Protocols:**
- **Mite Infestation:** Increase temperature to 32°C for 48 hours
- **Fungal Infection:** Isolate colony, improve ventilation
- **Bacterial Disease:** Remove affected substrate, probiotic treatment
- **Viral Outbreak:** Cull affected colony, deep clean facility

## 4. Harvesting Protocols

### 4.1 Harvest Timing
**Optimal Harvest Indicators:**
- Colony age: 6-12 months since establishment
- Colony size: >50,000 workers
- Fungus comb: Well-developed (if applicable)
- Season: Dry season preferred (higher fat content)

**Non-Destructive Harvesting (Preferred):**
- Harvest 20-30% of worker population
- Allow 3-4 weeks between harvests
- Monitor colony recovery between harvests
- Never harvest queen or royal court

### 4.2 Harvesting Methods
**Method 1: Surface Collection**
- Collect termites from foraging trails
- Use light traps at night (termites are phototropic)
- Collect from feeding stations
- Yield: 500-1000 grams per harvest

**Method 2: Nest Extraction**
- Access nest via tunnel openings
- Extract workers and soldiers from upper chambers
- Leave queen and brood intact
- Yield: 2-5 kg per harvest

**Method 3: Fungus Comb Harvest**
- Extract fungus comb from lower chambers
- Separate termites from comb material
- Return comb pieces for regeneration
- Yield: 1-3 kg fungus comb per harvest

### 4.3 Post-Harvest Colony Care
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Return any disturbed substrate
2. Provide fresh feeding material
3. Restore environmental conditions
4. Monitor for stress indicators

**Recovery Period:**
- Minimum 3 weeks between harvests
- Monitor worker population recovery
- Adjust feeding to support regrowth
- Record recovery metrics

## 5. Colony Expansion Protocol

### 5.1 Colony Splitting
**When to Split:**
- Colony size >100,000 workers
- Space limitations in current housing
- Production targets require expansion

**Splitting Procedure:**
1. Prepare new nest box with substrate
2. Transfer 40-50% of workers/brood
3. Include subordinate queen (if available)
4. Provide initial fungus comb pieces
5. Monitor both colonies for 30 days
6. Adjust resources based on performance

### 5.2 Breeding Program
**Selective Breeding Criteria:**
- High worker productivity
- Disease resistance
- Climate tolerance
- Fungus quality (if applicable)

**Record Keeping:**
- Colony performance metrics
- Genetic line tracking
- Production records
- Health history

## 7. Emergency Protocols

### 7.1 Equipment Failure
**Climate Control Failure:**
- Activate backup systems within 15 minutes
- Notify maintenance team immediately
- Monitor colony stress indicators
- Prepare emergency evacuation if needed

**Power Outage:**
- Generator activates automatically
- Battery backup for monitoring systems
- Manual ventilation procedures if extended outage

### 7.2 Colony Health Emergency
**Mass Mortality Event (>10% in 24 hours):**
1. Isolate affected colony immediately
2. Collect samples for laboratory analysis
3. Implement biosecurity protocols
4. Notify veterinarian/pathologist
5. Document all actions taken

**Pandemic Response (Farm-wide):**
1. Halt all colony movements
2. Implement enhanced PPE protocols
3. Increase environmental monitoring
4. Contact agricultural authorities
5. Consider temporary shutdown if severe
