# Quality Control Procedures for Termite Farming

## Executive Summary
This document establishes comprehensive quality control procedures for all aspects of termite farming operations, from colony management through final product distribution. Based on international food safety standards (HACCP, ISO 22000, SQF) with adaptations for insect farming specific requirements.

## 3. Colony Management Quality Control

### 3.1 Environmental Monitoring
**Temperature Monitoring:**
- Sensors: Digital with ±0.5°C accuracy
- Locations: 2 per colony house (top/bottom)
- Frequency: Continuous (data logged)
- Alerts: Automatic at ±2°C from setpoint
- Documentation: Daily reports, monthly trends

**Humidity Monitoring:**
- Sensors: Digital with ±2% RH accuracy
- Locations: 1 per colony house
- Frequency: Continuous (data logged)
- Alerts: Automatic at ±5% from setpoint
- Documentation: Daily reports, monthly trends

**Air Quality Monitoring:**
- CO₂: Continuous monitoring (<1000 ppm target)
- Ammonia: Weekly testing (<25 ppm target)
- Particulates: Monthly testing (MERV-13 filtration)
- Mold: Monthly spore counts

### 3.2 Colony Health Monitoring
**Daily Assessment:**
- Worker activity levels (count per minute)
- Surface trail presence/absence
- Fungus comb color (cream/tan = healthy)
- Brood presence (eggs, larvae, pupae)
- Waste disposal patterns

**Weekly Assessment:**
- Mortality rate (acceptable: <2% weekly)
- Colony size estimation
- Feeding station consumption
- Nest construction progress
- Parasite/disease inspection

**Monthly Assessment:**
- Comprehensive colony evaluation
- Performance metrics calculation
- Health trend analysis
- Resource optimization review

### 3.3 Colony Performance Metrics
| Metric | Target | Minimum Acceptable | Test Method |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| Worker survival rate | >95% monthly | >90% monthly | Mortality count |
| Colony growth rate | >10% monthly | >5% monthly | Size estimation |
| Harvest yield | 2 kg/month/colony | 1 kg/month/colony | Weight measurement |

### 5.2 Microbial Testing Protocol
**Routine Testing (Every Batch):**
1. **Total Plate Count (TPC)**
   - Method: Pour plate or spread plate
   - Incubation: 35°C, 48 hours
   - Acceptance: <10,000 CFU/g

2. **Coliforms/E. coli**
   - Method: Petrifilm or VRBA
   - Incubation: 35°C, 24 hours
   - Acceptance: Coliforms <10 CFU/g, E. coli <1 CFU/g

3. **Yeasts and Molds**
   - Method: Sabouraud dextrose agar
   - Incubation: 25°C, 5 days
   - Acceptance: <100 CFU/g

4. **Salmonella**
   - Method: PCR or cultural
   - Sample size: 25g
   - Acceptance: Absent

**Periodic Testing (Monthly/Quarterly):**
1. **Staphylococcus aureus**
   - Method: Baird-Parker agar
   - Acceptance: <10 CFU/g

2. **Clostridium perfringens**
   - Method: TSC agar
   - Acceptance: <100 CFU/g

3. **Listeria monocytogenes**
   - Method: PCR or cultural
   - Sample size: 25g
   - Acceptance: Absent

## 7. Non-Conformance Management

### 7.1 Non-Conformance Categories
**Category 1 (Critical):**
- Product safety issue
- Regulatory non-compliance
- Customer safety risk
- Potential recall situation

**Category 2 (Major):**
- Product specification failure
- Process deviation
- Equipment malfunction
- Documentation error

**Category 3 (Minor):**
- Cosmetic defect
- Minor process variation
- Documentation discrepancy
- Training gap identified

### 7.2 Non-Conformance Response
**Immediate Actions (All Categories):**
1. Identify and contain affected product
2. Document non-conformance
3. Notify appropriate personnel
4. Investigate root cause

**Corrective Actions:**
- Category 1: Immediate correction, management notification, regulatory reporting if required
- Category 2: Correction within 24 hours, root cause analysis, preventive action
- Category 3: Correction within 72 hours, trend analysis, process improvement

### 7.3 Root Cause Analysis Tools
**5-Why Analysis:**
- Ask "why" repeatedly until root cause identified
- Document each level of questioning
- Verify root cause is actionable

**Fishbone Diagram:**
- Man, Machine, Material, Method, Measurement, Environment
- Brainstorm potential causes
- Prioritize by impact and likelihood

**Pareto Analysis:**
- Chart non-conformances by category
- Focus on top 20% causing 80% of issues
- Track improvement over time
