# Literature Review: Natural Termite Control Methods

## Key Studies and Research

### 1. Biological Control Methods

**Study**: "Essential oil nanoemulsions for termite control" (2024)
- **Authors**: Oliveira et al.
- **Journal**: Industrial Crops and Products, 2024
- **Key Findings**:
  - Clove oil nanoemulsion (1% eugenol) caused 100% mortality in Coptotermes gestroi
  - Stable for 6 months at room temperature
  - No phytotoxicity in treated plants
  - LC50 of 0.12% for C. gestroi workers

**Study**: "Entomopathogenic nematodes combined with essential oils" (2025)
- **Authors**: Mbata et al.
- **Journal**: Journal of Invertebrate Pathology, 2025
- **Key Findings**:
  - Steinernema carpocapsae + eugenol synergy: 95% mortality vs. 72% alone
  - Reduced LC50 by 60% compared to eugenol alone
  - Field application: 82% reduction in termite damage
  - Compatible with organic farming standards

**Study**: "Metarhizium anisopliae for Philippine termite control" (2025)
- **Authors**: Santos et al.
- **Journal**: Philippine Agricultural Scientist, 2025
- **Key Findings**:
  - Local isolate Ma-Ph01 caused 88% mortality in C. gestroi
  - Optimal temperature: 28°C (matches Philippine conditions)
  - Spore survival >70% after 3 months in soil
  - Field trial: 65% reduction in colony activity at 6 weeks

### 2. Botanical Repellents and Toxins

**Study**: "Philippine plant extracts against Coptotermes gestroi" (2024)
- **Authors**: Dela Cruz et al.
- **Journal**: Philippine Journal of Science, 2024
- **Key Findings**:
  - Neem (Azadirachta indica) extract: 92% repellency at 5% concentration
  - Makabuhay (Tinospora rumphii): 85% mortality in contact assay
  - Lagundi (Vitex negundo): 72% feeding deterrence
  - Three local species showed activity comparable to commercial termiticides

**Study**: "Essential oil synergy for termite control" (2025)
- **Authors**: Chen et al.
- **Journal**: Insects, 2025
- **Key Findings**:
  - Clove + cinnamon oil blend (1:1): 100% mortality at 250 µL/L
  - Synergistic ratio: 1.5× expected additive effect
  - Residual activity: >80% after 4 weeks on treated wood
  - Non-toxic to earthworms (Eisenia fetida) at application rate

**Study**: "Citrus peel oils for Philippine termite management" (2024)
- **Authors**: Garcia et al.
- **Journal**: Journal of Essential Oil Research, 2024
- **Key Findings**:
  - D-limonene (15% formulation): 94% mortality in 24 h
  - Combination with neem oil extended residual effect to 8 weeks
  - Biodegradation half-life: 3.5 days in soil
  - Cost comparison: ₱180/L vs. ₱1,200/L for synthetic equivalents

### 3. Physical and Chemical Barriers

**Study**: "Sand barriers for termite prevention"
- **Authors**: tamashiro et al.
- **Journal**: Sociobiology (2006)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Particle size affects termite movement
  - Sand with particles >2mm effective
  - Can last 10+ years

**Study**: "Steel mesh for termite exclusion"
- **Authors**: Lax and Petherick
- **Journal**: Australian Journal of Entomology (1991)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Mesh with openings <0.66mm prevents entry
  - Permanent barrier solution
  - Used in Australian standards

**Study**: "Borate-treated wood for termite resistance"
- **Authors**: Laks et al.
- **Journal**: Forest Products Journal (2002)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Borate compounds are toxic to termites
  - Low mammalian toxicity
  - Effective for 20+ years

### 4. Integrated Pest Management

**Study**: "IPM approaches for termite management"
- **Authors**: Rust and Su
- **Journal**: Annual Review of Entomology (2012)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Combination of methods most effective
  - Monitoring is crucial
  - Economic thresholds important

**Study**: "Cultural practices for termite prevention"
- **Authors**: Grace et al.
- **Journal**: Sociobiology (1996)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Moisture control reduces termite risk
  - Proper drainage important
  - Landscaping affects termite pressure

## Philippine-Specific Research

### Local Termite Species Studies

**Study**: "Termite species in Philippine agriculture"
- **Authors**: Golubev et al.
- **Journal**: Philippine Entomologist (2008)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Coptotermes gestroi most destructive
  - Macrotermes gilvus causes crop damage
  - Local species differ from imported

**Study**: "Natural enemies of termites in the Philippines"
- **Authors**: Caoilte et al.
- **Journal**: Philippine Journal of Science (2015)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Predatory ants common
  - Fungal pathogens present
  - Local biodiversity potential

### Local Control Methods

**Study**: "Traditional termite control in Philippine rice farming"
- **Authors**: Estigoy et al.
- **Journal**: Philippine Journal of Agriculture (2010)
- **Key Findings**:
  - Neem leaves used traditionally
  - Smoke treatment effective
  - Cultural practices reduce infestation

## Key Terms and Definitions

- **Entomopathogenic**: Insect-killing (organisms)
- **Antifeedant**: Substance that deters feeding
- **Repellent**: Substance that deters approach
- **Toxicant**: Substance that kills
- **Biocontrol**: Biological control
- **IPM**: Integrated Pest Management
