## Strain Characteristics

### Cellulase Activity Classification

**High Activity**:
- Clear zone diameter > 20 mm on CMC agar
- Specific activity > 50 U/mg protein
- Cellulose degradation > 80% in 72 hours

**Medium Activity**:
- Clear zone diameter 10-20 mm on CMC agar
- Specific activity 20-50 U/mg protein
- Cellulose degradation 50-80% in 72 hours

**Low Activity**:
- Clear zone diameter < 10 mm on CMC agar
- Specific activity < 20 U/mg protein
- Cellulose degradation < 50% in 72 hours

### Growth Characteristics

**Fast Growing**:
- Doubling time < 2 hours
- Colony formation in < 24 hours

**Slow Growing**:
- Doubling time > 4 hours
- Colony formation in > 48 hours

### Storage Requirements

**Short-term**:
- 4°C for 1-2 weeks
- Room temperature for 1-2 days

**Long-term**:
- -80°C in 15% glycerol
- Liquid nitrogen for permanent storage

## Isolation Protocols

### 1. Primary Isolation

**Materials**:
- Cellulose agar plates
- Anaerobic chamber
- Incubator at 30°C

**Procedure**:
1. Prepare cellulose agar plates
2. Dilute gut extract (10^-1 to 10^-6)
3. Plate on cellulose agar
4. Incubate anaerobically at 30°C for 5-7 days
5. Pick colonies with clear zones
6. Purify by streaking

### 2. Purification

**Materials**:
- Fresh cellulose agar plates
- Sterile inoculating loops
- Anaerobic chamber

**Procedure**:
1. Pick isolated colony
2. Streak on fresh plate for isolation
3. Incubate anaerobically at 30°C for 3-5 days
4. Pick single colony
5. Repeat until pure culture obtained
