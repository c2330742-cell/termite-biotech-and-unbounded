# Safety Guidelines for Biofuel Research Lab

## General Laboratory Safety

### 1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**Required PPE**:
- Lab coat (buttoned at all times)
- Safety glasses or goggles
- Closed-toe shoes
- Gloves (when handling chemicals or biological materials)

**Additional PPE**:
- Face shield (when working with hazardous chemicals)
- Respirator (when working with aerosols or volatile chemicals)
- Heat-resistant gloves (when working with hot equipment)

### 2. Lab Conduct

**Do**:
- Keep work areas clean and organized
- Wash hands before and after lab work
- Report spills and accidents immediately
- Follow standard operating procedures
- Use equipment properly

**Don't**:
- Eat, drink, or smoke in the lab
- Apply cosmetics
- Store food in lab refrigerators
- Work alone in the lab (minimum 2 people)
- Rush through procedures

### 3. Chemical Safety

**Chemical Storage**:
- Store chemicals by compatibility class
- Keep flammables in approved cabinets
- Label all containers clearly
- Keep secondary containers for corrosives
- Maintain chemical inventory

**Chemical Handling**:
- Read Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before use
- Use fume hoods for volatile chemicals
- Never pipette by mouth
- Dispose of chemicals properly
- Clean up spills immediately

**Chemical Disposal**:
- Segregate waste by type
- Label waste containers clearly
- Never pour chemicals down the drain
- Arrange for professional disposal
- Document disposal in log book

### 4. Biological Safety

**Biosafety Levels**:
- BSL-1: Low-risk organisms (non-pathogenic)
- BSL-2: Moderate-risk organisms (some pathogens)
- BSL-3: High-risk organisms (serious pathogens)
- BSL-4: Highest-risk organisms (dangerous pathogens)

**For this project**: BSL-1 and BSL-2 organisms will be used

**Biological Safety Procedures**:
- Work in designated areas
- Use appropriate biosafety cabinet
- Autoclave all biological waste
- Decontaminate work surfaces
- Document all biological materials

**Specific Guidelines for Termite Research**:
- Handle termites with forceps
- Avoid contact with soldier mandibles
- Wear gloves when handling gut extracts
- Autoclave all biological materials
- Store cultures at appropriate temperatures

### 5. Equipment Safety

**General Equipment Rules**:
- Read equipment manuals before use
- Follow startup/shutdown procedures
- Never leave equipment unattended when running
- Report malfunctions immediately
- Perform regular maintenance

**Specific Equipment**:

**Autoclave**:
- Never open when hot
- Use heat-resistant gloves
- Allow to cool before opening
- Check pressure gauge regularly
- Document each cycle

**Centrifuge**:
- Balance loads properly
- Never exceed maximum speed
- Wait for complete stop before opening
- Check for damaged tubes before use
- Clean spills immediately

**Bioreactor**:
- Monitor temperature and pH regularly
- Check for leaks before operation
- Never exceed recommended volumes
- Have emergency shutdown procedure
- Document all parameters

**Gas Chromatograph**:
- Handle gas cylinders carefully
- Check for leaks regularly
- Never use without proper ventilation
- Follow manufacturer guidelines
- Document maintenance

### 6. Fire Safety

**Prevention**:
- Keep flammable chemicals in approved cabinets
- Never use open flames near flammables
- Keep electrical equipment in good condition
- Don't overload electrical outlets
- Keep exits clear

**Response**:
- Know location of fire extinguishers
- Know how to use fire extinguishers (PASS technique)
- Have evacuation plan posted
- Practice fire drills regularly
- Report fire hazards immediately

**Fire Extinguisher Types**:
- Class A: Ordinary combustibles
- Class B: Flammable liquids
- Class C: Electrical fires
- Class D: Metal fires
- Class K: Kitchen fires

### 7. Emergency Procedures

**Spill Response**:
1. Alert others in the area
2. Contain spill if safe to do so
3. Notify lab supervisor
4. Clean up using appropriate protocols
5. Document incident

**Chemical Spill**:
- Small spill: Clean up with appropriate absorbent
- Large spill: Evacuate and call emergency response
- Toxic spill: Evacuate and call emergency response

**Biological Spill**:
- Cover with absorbent material
- Apply disinfectant
- Wait appropriate contact time
- Clean up and autoclave materials
- Document incident

**Exposure Response**:
- Skin contact: Wash with soap and water for 15 minutes
- Eye contact: Flush with water for 15 minutes
- Inhalation: Move to fresh air
- Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting, seek medical attention
- Document incident and seek medical attention

**Injury Response**:
- Minor injury: First aid and document
- Major injury: Call emergency services
- Always document incidents
- Follow up with medical care

### 8. Waste Management

**Biological Waste**:
- Autoclave all biological materials
- Use biohazard bags
- Label clearly
- Arrange for proper disposal
- Document disposal

**Chemical Waste**:
- Segregate by type
- Label clearly with contents
- Store in designated areas
- Arrange for professional disposal
- Document disposal

**Sharps Disposal**:
- Use designated sharps containers
- Do not overfill
- Arrange for professional disposal
- Document disposal

**Radioactive Waste** (if applicable):
- Follow radiation safety protocols
- Use designated containers
- Document all radioactive materials
- Arrange for proper disposal

### 9. Training Requirements

**Required Training**:
- Lab safety orientation
- Chemical safety training
- Biological safety training
- Equipment-specific training
- Emergency response training

**Documentation**:
- Maintain training records
- Update training annually
- Document competency assessments
- Keep training certificates on file

## Specific Safety Procedures for Termite Biofuel Research

### 1. Termite Handling Safety

**Collection**:
- Wear gloves when collecting termites
- Use forceps to handle termites
- Avoid contact with soldier mandibles
- Place in appropriate containers
- Transport securely to lab

**Lab Handling**:
- Work in designated area
- Wear gloves and safety glasses
- Use forceps or brushes
- Avoid direct contact
- Document all handling

### 2. Gut Extract Safety

**Preparation**:
- Work in biosafety cabinet
- Wear gloves and lab coat
- Use sterile technique
- Avoid aerosol generation
- Clean up spills immediately

**Storage**:
- Store at appropriate temperature
- Label clearly
- Use secondary containment
- Monitor for contamination
- Document storage conditions

### 3. Bacterial Culture Safety

**Handling**:
- Work in biosafety cabinet
- Use sterile technique
- Avoid aerosol generation
- Decontaminate work area
- Document all cultures

**Storage**:
- Store at appropriate temperature
- Label clearly
- Use secondary containment
- Monitor for contamination
- Document storage conditions

### 4. Bioreactor Safety

**Operation**:
- Monitor temperature and pH
- Check for leaks regularly
- Never exceed recommended volumes
- Have emergency shutdown procedure
- Document all parameters

**Maintenance**:
- Follow manufacturer guidelines
- Perform regular maintenance
- Document all maintenance
- Train staff on procedures
- Have backup systems

### 5. Chemical Safety for Enzyme Assays

**DNS Reagent**:
- Contains 3,5-dinitrosalicylic acid
- Avoid skin contact
- Use in fume hood
- Dispose properly
- Document usage

**Other Chemicals**:
- Read SDS before use
- Use appropriate PPE
- Handle in fume hood
- Dispose properly
- Document usage
