# Laboratory Protocols for Termite Biofuel Research

## Standard Operating Procedures

### 1. Termite Collection and Handling

**Materials Needed**:
- Collection containers (plastic vials with moist paper towels)
- Forceps and brushes
- Cooler with ice packs
- Field notebook

**Procedure**:
1. Identify termite mounds or infested wood
2. Collect workers and soldiers using forceps
3. Place in containers with moist paper towels
4. Transport to lab within 24 hours
5. Store at 25°C until processing

**Safety Notes**:
- Wear gloves when handling termites
- Avoid contact with soldier mandibles
- Work in well-ventilated area

### 2. Gut Extraction Protocol

**Equipment**:
- Dissection microscope
- Micro-dissection tools
- Sterile saline solution (0.85% NaCl)
- Microcentrifuge tubes

**Procedure**:
1. Anesthetize termites at 4°C for 10 minutes
2. Surface-sterilize with 70% ethanol
3. Place under dissection microscope
4. Remove gut using sterile forceps
5. Place gut in saline solution
6. Homogenize using sterile pestle
7. Centrifuge at 10,000 rpm for 10 minutes
8. Collect supernatant (gut extract)

**Storage**:
- Store at -80°C for long-term
- Use within 1 week for fresh extracts

### 3. Bacterial Isolation Protocol

**Media Preparation**:
- cellulose agar plates
- Anaerobic chamber conditions
- Incubation at 30°C

**Procedure**:
1. Dilute gut extract in sterile saline (10^-1 to 10^-6)
2. Plate on cellulose agar
3. Incubate anaerobically at 30°C for 5-7 days
4. Pick colonies showing clear zones (cellulase activity)
5. Purify by streaking on fresh plates
6. Store isolates at -80°C in glycerol stocks

### 4. Cellulase Activity Assay

**Materials**:
- CMC (carboxymethyl cellulose) substrate
- DNS reagent (3,5-dinitrosalicylic acid)
- Spectrophotometer
- Incubator at 50°C

**Procedure**:
1. Prepare 1% CMC solution in sodium acetate buffer (pH 5.0)
2. Add 1 mL enzyme extract to 9 mL substrate
3. Incubate at 50°C for 30 minutes
4. Add 1 mL DNS reagent to stop reaction
5. Boil for 5 minutes
6. Cool and measure absorbance at 540 nm
7. Calculate cellulase activity (units/mg protein)

**One Unit** = Amount of enzyme releasing 1 μmol glucose per minute

### 5. Ethanol Production Protocol

**Fermentation Setup**:
- 250 mL Erlenmeyer flasks
- Cellulose substrate (1-5% w/v)
- Inoculum: 10% (v/v) bacterial culture
- Anaerobic conditions

**Procedure**:
1. Prepare cellulose substrate in fermentation medium
2. Autoclave at 121°C for 15 minutes
3. Cool to room temperature
4. Add bacterial inoculum
5. Incubate at 30°C for 48-72 hours
6. Sample at 12-hour intervals
7. Analyze ethanol content by gas chromatography

### 6. Ethanol Analysis (Gas Chromatography)

**Equipment**:
- Gas chromatograph with FID detector
- DB-WAX column (30m x 0.32mm)
- Injection volume: 1 μL

**Conditions**:
- Carrier gas: Helium at 1 mL/min
- Injector temperature: 200°C
- Detector temperature: 250°C
- Oven program: 40°C (5 min) → 10°C/min → 200°C (5 min)

**Calibration**:
- Prepare ethanol standards (0-10% v/v)
- Create calibration curve
- Calculate sample concentration

### 7. DNA Extraction Protocol

**Materials**:
- DNA extraction kit (Qiagen or equivalent)
- Lysis buffer
- Proteinase K
- Ethanol

**Procedure**:
1. Pellet 1 mL bacterial culture
2. Resuspend in lysis buffer
3. Add proteinase K and incubate at 56°C for 1 hour
4. Follow kit instructions for DNA purification
5. Quantify using NanoDrop spectrophotometer
6. Store at -20°C

## Quality Control

### Daily Checks
- Monitor temperature logs
- Check equipment calibration
- Verify media sterility
- Document observations

### Weekly Checks
- Backup cultures
- Clean and sterilize equipment
- Review experimental records
- Update inventory

### Monthly Checks
- Equipment maintenance
- Safety equipment checks
- Protocol reviews
- Data analysis review

## Waste Disposal

### Biological Waste
- Autoclave all biological materials
- Dispose in designated biohazard bags
- Document disposal in log book

### Chemical Waste
- Segregate by chemical type
- Label clearly with contents
- Store in designated areas
- Arrange for proper disposal

### Sharps Disposal
- Use designated sharps containers
- Do not overfill
- Arrange for professional disposal

## Emergency Procedures

### Spills
1. Contain spill immediately
2. Notify lab supervisor
3. Clean using appropriate protocols
4. Document incident

### Exposure
1. Wash affected area immediately
2. Seek medical attention if needed
3. Document incident
4. Report to safety officer

### Equipment Failure
1. Secure samples immediately
2. Notify technical support
3. Document failure
4. Arrange for repairs or replacement

## Documentation Requirements

### Lab Notebook
- Date and time of all activities
- Detailed procedures followed
- Observations and results
- Deviations from protocol
- Signatures and witness marks

### Digital Records
- Back up data weekly
- Maintain version control
- Store in secure location
- Access restricted to authorized personnel
